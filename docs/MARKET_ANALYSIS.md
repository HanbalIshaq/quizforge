# Market Analysis — Online Assessment & Live-Polling Platforms

A study of the leading paid platforms and their feature offerings, conducted to define a feature parity / differentiation strategy for QuizForge.

## 1. The competitive landscape

The market splits into four overlapping segments. QuizForge competes across all four — which is both an opportunity (one tool replaces several) and a challenge (each segment has specialists).

| Segment | Leaders | Core use case | Typical buyer |
|---|---|---|---|
| **K-12 / classroom quizzes** | Quizizz, Kahoot!, Blooket, Gimkit, Quizalize | Engagement, gamification, formative checks | Teachers, schools |
| **Higher-ed & corporate testing** | ClassMarker, Easy LMS, ProProfs, FlexiQuiz | Formal exams, certification, anti-cheating | Trainers, HR, certifying bodies |
| **Audience engagement / live polls** | Mentimeter, Slido, Wooclap, Poll Everywhere | Live presentations, conferences, Q&A | Speakers, meeting hosts |
| **Pre-hire & skills assessment** | TestGorilla, iMocha, Vervoe, Mettl, Codility | Candidate screening | Recruiters, hiring managers |

## 2. Detailed competitor breakdown

### Quizizz
- **Pricing (2026):** Basic free; Super $7.95/teacher/mo; Premium $9.95/teacher/mo; School & District tiers (custom quote, typically $4-6/student/yr at scale).
- **Standout features:** AI quiz generator (from doc/PDF/URL), 70+ languages, 14+ question types incl. drag-and-drop, memes/music, paper mode (offline OMR), accommodations (text-to-speech, font sizing), large public library (millions of quizzes), Google Classroom + Canvas + Schoology integrations.
- **Limitations:** Designed for K-12 vibe; less suited to formal certification.

### Kahoot!
- **Pricing (2026):** Kahoot! Basic free; Kahoot!+ Start $3.99/mo; Kahoot!+ Premier $7.99/mo; Education plans starting $17/teacher/mo; Business plans $17-31/host/mo.
- **Standout features:** Game-show live mode with music + leaderboard, team mode, "AI question generator," "Kahoot! Academy" template library, "Kahoot! Audio Stories," premium reports & league system, mobile apps (iOS/Android).
- **Limitations:** Question variety still limited (heavily MCQ); not for high-stakes testing.

### ClassMarker
- **Pricing (2026):** Pay-as-you-go from $25/100 tests; Monthly subscriptions $24.95–$199.95/mo by volume; Yearly heavily discounted.
- **Standout features:** Formal exam focus: certificates with serial numbers and verification URLs, time limits, randomized question selection from banks, plagiarism prevention (right-click block, copy/paste block), white-label option, REST API, webhook events, multiple-instructor accounts, group management, anti-cheating (IP restrictions, password protection), branch by score, SCORM/xAPI export.
- **Limitations:** UI looks 2010s; no live mode; no question types beyond standard exam set.

### Mentimeter
- **Pricing (2026):** Free (limited Qs per presentation); Basic $11.99/mo; Pro $24.99/mo; Enterprise custom.
- **Standout features:** Slide-deck format with interactive Qs interleaved, 13 question types incl. word cloud, scales, ranking, open text, Wheel, Heat-map, branding/themes, comments & reactions, integrations (PowerPoint, Teams, Zoom).
- **Limitations:** Presentation-shaped; not built for graded assessments.

### Slido (Cisco)
- **Pricing (2026):** Free for up to 100 participants; Engage $12.50/mo; Professional $50/mo; Enterprise.
- **Standout features:** Q&A focus (upvoting), polls during meetings, Zoom/Teams/Webex/Google Meet native integration, moderation, anonymous mode, post-event analytics.
- **Limitations:** Lightweight on quiz creation; not designed for testing.

### ProProfs Quiz Maker
- **Pricing (2026):** Free tier limited; Essentials $20/mo; Business $40/mo; Forever $499 one-time.
- **Standout features:** 15+ question types (incl. hotspot, drag-drop, drawing), AI quiz generator, 100k+ pre-built question library, certificates, anti-cheating, white-label, lead-gen forms after a quiz, e-commerce (paywall a quiz), LMS integration, multi-language, screen recording.
- **Limitations:** UI dated; pricing climbs fast at scale.

### TestGorilla
- **Pricing (2026):** Free 5 candidates/mo; Starter ~$9/job/mo; Pro ~$10K/yr enterprise.
- **Standout features:** 400+ ready-made tests (cognitive, role-specific, language, software), anti-cheating (webcam snapshots, single tab, full-screen lock, IP, copy-paste tracking), ATS integrations (50+), candidate-friendly experience.
- **Limitations:** Hiring-only; not for general quizzes.

### Edulastic
- **Pricing (2026):** Free basic; Premium $100/teacher/yr; District licensing.
- **Standout features:** 60+ tech-enhanced item types (math equation editor, graphing, hot-spot, label-image, chemical formula), aligned to Common Core/state standards, rosters & classes, longitudinal mastery tracking, parent reports.
- **Limitations:** US K-12 focus; less useful outside that segment.

### Formative (GoFormative)
- **Pricing (2026):** Free basic; Essentials $15/teacher/mo; Premium $20/teacher/mo.
- **Standout features:** Real-time monitoring of every student's screen as they work, draw/markup on images, audio response questions, "show your work" canvas, instant feedback.
- **Limitations:** No live game-show mode.

### Wooclap
- **Pricing (2026):** Free for ≤2 Qs; Basic ~$10/mo; Premium ~$20/mo; School licenses.
- **Standout features:** 20+ question types including brainstorm and find-on-image, PowerPoint/Google Slides plugins, LMS integrations, multilingual support, attendance.
- **Limitations:** Live-engagement focus; weaker async testing.

### FlexiQuiz
- **Pricing (2026):** Free limited; Starter $25/mo; Premium $45/mo; Pro $90/mo; Enterprise.
- **Standout features:** Branching/conditional logic, white-label, custom domains, certificate templates, password-protected quizzes, exam timer with proctoring snapshots, e-commerce (charge per quiz), Zapier integration.
- **Limitations:** No real-time live mode; rendering on mobile inconsistent.

## 5. Where QuizForge stands today

**Strengths**
- 10 question types, including word-cloud and rating that even some paid tools lack.
- Both live game-show mode AND async share-link mode in one tool.
- Real-time host dashboard with bar-chart aggregates + reveal + leaderboard.
- Auto-grading + manual-grading for essays.
- Bulk import from .docx/.csv/.txt (matches Quizizz/ClassMarker).
- CSV + Excel export.
- Self-hostable. Data stays with the customer.
- Modern UI (Tailwind), mobile-responsive.

**Gaps vs paid competitors (priority for SaaS readiness)**
- ⚠️ No multi-tenant org/team model (every user is independent).
- ⚠️ No roles (admin/teacher/student); first signup currently equals teacher.
- ⚠️ No anti-cheating (copy-paste block, tab tracking, full-screen, webcam).
- ⚠️ No certificate generation with verification URL.
- ⚠️ No image/hotspot/matching/ordering/drag-drop question types.
- ⚠️ No equation/math editor.
- ⚠️ No question bank reuse / categories / tags.
- ⚠️ No per-question time limit (only quiz-wide).
- ⚠️ No AI quiz generation (huge differentiator now).
- ⚠️ No public API or webhooks.
- ⚠️ No LMS integration (LTI 1.3 / SCORM export).
- ⚠️ No mobile apps.
- ⚠️ No custom branding / white-label / custom domain.
- ⚠️ No public quiz library / templates.
- ⚠️ No multi-language UI.
- ⚠️ No paywall / lead-gen.

## 6. Differentiation strategy (the "why QuizForge")

We will not beat Quizizz on K-12 polish or Kahoot on game-show gloss; those are entrenched. Three credible angles to win:

1. **Self-hosted with full data ownership** — schools and corporates with data-residency or privacy rules (GDPR, HIPAA-adjacent, government, banking) cannot use SaaS. ClassMarker offers a similar story but is closed-source and pricey. QuizForge can be open-core and licensed.
2. **One tool for both live + async + certification** — no other tool covers Kahoot's game show AND ClassMarker's formal exam AND Mentimeter's polls in one polished product. Most teams buy 2-3 tools.
3. **AI-native** — newer entrant advantage: bake AI quiz generation, AI grading hints, AI feedback comments, and AI proctoring (camera anomaly detection) into core features rather than retrofitting.
